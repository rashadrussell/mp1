Hacks

PearlEdit is a live WYSIWYG layout editor that allows for in-browser customization while guaranteeing responsive themes. The goal of PearlEdit is to give developers who do not have a knack for design an easy way to produce beautiful layouts. PearlEdit was a project that I initialized as an intern on the YUI team at Yahoo during the summer of 2013. The Pure CSS framework was released on the first day of my internship, and I was amazed of its ability to allow developers to rapidly create responsive themes. I wanted to create a project that capitalizes on that ability as well as promote its existance. The intent of PearlEdit's user experience was to give users the ability to design a layout directly as it would appear, live in the browser. I wanted to provide editing tools that are made from a user interface designer's perspective such as premade slideshows, forms, color palettes, and icons. Overall, the hope is for PearlEdit to enable users to choose premade responsive themes with the proper tools that will give non-designers the power to generate beautiful interfaces.
